05 Green Flags & Red Flags: What Actually Worked vs. What Failed О“ГњГ¦ Verdict
в‰ЎЖ’Ж’Гі Green Flags О“Г‡Г¶ What Showed Evidence of Impact
MGNREGA (2005): Empirically linked to ~40% reduction in farmer suicides between 2010О“Г‡Гґ2019. Kerala: 1,118 О“ГҐГ† 105. West Bengal: zero by 2012. World's largest rural employment guarantee. Reduced emergency debt from moneylenders.
Kisan Credit Card (KCC, 1998): Reduced moneylender dependence for short-term credit. 7.75 Cr operational accounts (Mar 2024). Institutional share of agri credit grew from ~40% (1990) to ~69.6% (2024).
Foodgrain Production Growth: From 209 MT (FY00) to 357.73 MT (FY25) О“Г‡Г¶ a 71% increase over 25 years. Rice and wheat at all-time records. Self-sufficiency in most staples achieved.
Agricultural Export Growth: From ~$8B (FY00) to $51.9B (FY25) О“Г‡Г¶ a 6в”њГ№ increase. India now 9th globally in agri exports. Rice export at $12.47B О“Г‡Г¶ record high.
PM-KISAN Income Support: О“Г©в•Ј3.46 lakh crore transferred to 11 crore farmers in 6 years. Direct benefit transfer (DBT) with minimal leakage. Added income floor for small/marginal farmers.
Irrigated Area Expansion: From ~55 Mha (FY00) to 75.3 Mha (FY25) О“Г‡Г¶ +37% over 25 years. PMKSY and micro-irrigation schemes reduced weather vulnerability in targeted regions.
в‰ЎЖ’Г¶в”¤ Red Flags О“Г‡Г¶ What Failed Despite Massive Spending

Debt Waiver (2008, О“Г©в•Ј71,000 Cr): Largest ever. But one-time only. Did not prevent re-indebtedness. By 2018О“Г‡Гґ19, average outstanding loan back to О“Г©в•Ј74,121/household. Waivers treated symptoms; ignored root causes.

F1
Land Fragmentation Crisis (Unaddressed by All Governments): Average landholding has shrunk from 2.3 ha (1960) to ~1.07 ha (2024). At this size, mechanisation is economically unviable, input costs per unit of output are higher, and risk absorption capacity is near zero. 86% of India's 140 million farm households are small/marginal. No government in 26 years has introduced meaningful land consolidation policy, cooperative cultivation incentives, or economies-of-scale models at scale. This is the foundational structural problem that every budget line ignores.
F2
MSP: The 57-Year-Old Promise Never Kept (Across All Governments): MSP has been announced for 23 crops since 1966. Effective government procurement exists only for wheat and rice. For 20+ other crops О“Г‡Г¶ pulses, oilseeds, millets, cotton О“Г‡Г¶ farmers must sell in mandis at market rates that frequently fall below MSP. Swaminathan Commission (2006) recommended legal guarantee of C2+50% MSP. Neither UPA-II nor NDA-II/III has implemented this. Farm Laws (2020), which could have expanded market access, were repealed after protests О“Г‡Г¶ without replacing them with the statutory MSP guarantee that farmers demanded.
F3
Post-Harvest Loss Economy (О“Г©в•Ј1 Lakh Crore Annual Destruction): India loses ~40% of horticultural produce and 5О“Г‡Гґ7% of foodgrains annually to post-harvest losses (APEDA/ICAR). This represents ~О“Г©в•Ј90,000О“Г‡Гґ1,00,000 crore of value destruction per year О“Г‡Г¶ approximately equal to 75% of the entire agriculture ministry budget. Cold chain infrastructure covers less than 5% of perishable output. Despite multiple schemes (MIDH, PMKSY, AIF), the cold chain gap has not been systematically addressed in any government's term. The produce of the farmer is destroyed before it reaches the market.
F4
Input Cost Inflation (Unchecked for 26 Years): Fertiliser prices rose 300О“Г‡Гґ400% between 2000 and 2024 (DAP price: О“Г©в•Ј4,830/bag in FY00 to О“Г©в•Ј27,000+/bag in FY23 before partial subsidy rollback). Seed costs up 150О“Г‡Гґ200%. Diesel 180% (direct farm machinery cost). MSP revisions have not kept pace with the C2 cost of cultivation for most crops. Every government has subsidised inputs О“Г‡Г¶ but subsidies consistently lag cost increases, and subsidy efficiency is poor (broad-acre fertiliser subsidy benefits large farmers disproportionately).
F5
Regional Credit Inequality (A Structural NABARD Failure): Southern India (17% of cropped area) receives 48% of total agricultural credit. Eastern and Central India О“Г‡Г¶ Bihar, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, eastern UP О“Г‡Г¶ where small/marginal farmer concentration is highest, receive a disproportionately small share. The KCC scheme, while nationally scaled, is effectively concentrated in states with stronger banking infrastructure. The farmers most in need of formal credit remain most dependent on informal moneylenders charging 24О“Г‡Гґ36% annual interest.
F6
NCRB Data Suppression (2016О“Г‡Гґ2019): The National Crime Records Bureau did not publish its "Accidental Deaths & Suicides in India" report for the years 2016, 2017, and 2018 for nearly three years, releasing consolidated data only in 2019О“Г‡Гґ2020. This is not an administrative delay О“Г‡Г¶ this is the most widely cited source of farmer suicide data, and its suppression during a period of intensifying agrarian distress (farm loan waivers, Vidarbha crisis sequel, input cost spikes) constitutes a governance accountability failure. Transparency in crisis data is itself a policy obligation.

India's public health spend: Only ~2.1% of GDP (2021О“Г‡Гґ22, NHA) О“Г‡Г¶ among the lowest in G20. WHO recommends minimum 5%. Rural primary health centres face 30О“Г‡Гґ40% doctor vacancy rates. The burden falls on household savings О“Г‡Г¶ which farmers do not have.
в‰ЎЖ’Ж’Гі Rural MPCE: What Did Improve (Conditionally)
Nominal 8в”њГ№ growth: Rural MPCE grew from О“Г©в•Ј486 (1999О“Г‡Гґ00) to О“Г©в•Ј3,773 (2022О“Г‡Гґ23) О“Г‡Г¶ a near 8-fold nominal increase over 23 years.

The Composite Welfare Picture (Across All 26 Years): Rural Indians are consuming more, earning more nominally, and are less likely to be in extreme poverty than in 2000. However: their debt has grown 16в”њГ№ (О“Г©в•Ј7,539 О“ГҐГ† О“Г©в•Ј1,25,000 per household), their productivity gap versus urban workers has widened, their health expenditures remain catastrophic when illness strikes, and the consumption gap between rural and urban India has grown from 43% to 71%. The floor has risen; the ceiling has not. Agricultural policy has successfully prevented famine but has failed to engineer prosperity.

09 Conclusions: The System Is Not Broken О“Г‡Г¶ It Is Working As Designed О“ГњГ¦ Verdict
The Central Finding of This Report:

India's agricultural policy system over 26 years has been extraordinarily effective at one thing: producing macro-level metrics that satisfy political communication needs О“Г‡Г¶ record production, rising budgets, growing exports О“Г‡Г¶ while preserving the structural conditions that ensure farming remains economically unviable for 86% of farmers. This is not malice. It is institutional capture by the most tractable variables: output, credit disbursement, budget size. The hardest variables О“Г‡Г¶ land fragmentation, price realisation, post-harvest infrastructure, effective MSP, rural wage growth О“Г‡Г¶ remain unaddressed across all three governments because they require structural reform that disrupts existing power configurations (traders, intermediaries, fertiliser industry, large-farm lobbies).

Every rupee spent on schemes without fixing these structural variables is a rupee that diminishes in impact.
C1
Budget О“Г«ГЎ Impact: The 580% increase in agricultural budget from FY00 to FY26 has not produced a proportional improvement in farmer welfare. Real farm household income grew ~40О“Г‡Гґ50% over 25 years; urban informal income grew ~120%. The rural-urban income gap is wider today than in FY2000.
C2
MGNREGA is the Most Effective Agricultural Policy in 26 Years: Not a farming scheme, not a credit scheme, not an insurance scheme О“Г‡Г¶ a wage employment scheme О“Г‡Г¶ is the most empirically validated intervention in reducing agricultural distress. This tells us something profound about what farmers actually need: income certainty, not subsidised inputs.
C3
Record Production and Persistent Suicide Coexist Because They Measure Different Things: Foodgrain output measures aggregate national production. Farmer welfare measures individual household income, debt, and livelihood security. The former has been the policy objective. The latter is the human reality. Until policy is evaluated against welfare outcomes rather than production metrics, this divergence will persist.
C4
Data Transparency Itself Is a Policy Variable: The suppression of NCRB farmer suicide data for 2016О“Г‡Гґ2019, the absence of a universal crop-loss compensation database, and the non-publication of effective (not announced) MSP realisation data across crops О“Г‡Г¶ these are not administrative oversights. They are governance choices that protect policy from accountability.
